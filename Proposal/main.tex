\chapter{Introduction}

Financial markets are highly complex systems that are influenced by the behaviour of humans and economic events. For decades data scientists and economicals have attempted to predict and explain financial time series, but it still remains a big challenge. Traditionally, autoregressive models or technical indicators were used to achieve this goal. While this approach created a mathematical and statistical foundation, it falls short when confronted with unstructured data like news headlines or investor sentiment.

With the rapid advances of artificial intelligence (AI), especially in natural language processing (NLP), new ways for understanding those unstructured texts have been found. For example, transformer models can extract semantic information from text. This information could be used to engineer new features that help explain variance in the movement of stock market indices like the S\&P500 or the MSCI World.

The goal of this master thesis, therefore, is to investigate if a stochastic forecasting approach can be improved by adding sentiment-based features extracted from news headlines and/or social media posts. As a proof of concept (PoC), the work will analyse an ARMA model with and without sentiment integration. Later, more complex AI models – ranging from linear regression and XGBoost to recurrent neural networks (RNN), long short-term memory networks (LSTM), and state-of-the-art transformer models like GPT – will be evaluated and benchmarked. To analyse performance, metrics such as Mean Percentage Error (MPE), Accuracy, and F1-Score will be used.

This investigation contributes to an ongoing debate about the efficient market hypothesis, which states that new information is integrated into prices in a very short time, rendering the information useless for predicting future movements.

\chapter{Research Problem and Goals}
\section{Research Problem}

Despite decades-long research in terms of stock predictions, the prognosis of short-term movements remains a challenge. Classic methods depend solely on previous price movements and technical indicators. However, the influential factors that move the market go far beyond these information. News, Social Media, politics, macro-economic events are examples that could contribute to changes in the stock market as well.

Current studies increasingly work with the analysis of media texts to achieve additional prediction-accuracy, though, the quantitative value of these features is debated. Additionally, the interaction between technical indicators and sentiment features — and their impact on model performance across different AI architectures — remains an open question.
\section{Goals}
The goal of this study is to quantify the benefit of market-sentiment to predict changes in stock prices. Different AI-algorithms will be compared and analysed on which would benefit the most from this new feature.

Specifically this study tries to:

1. create a pipeline to collect and combine news articles. Multiple different datasets that contain american news headlines will be combined. The goal is to create a bigger volume of data to create the sentiment feature.

2. take the data and calculate the sentiment score (1: positiv, -1: negativ, 0: neutral) with different methods and models. There will be experiments on which of the algorithms delivers the best results. For Example a lexicon based approach or a state of the art fine tuned transformer models can be used.

3. compare the difference of sentiment-lag (-day-lag vs 1-day-lag, intra-day-lag) to analyse the decay of explainability over time.

4. implement a pipeline for extracting technical indicators from the stock market prices and combining tehm with the sentiment score. The output should be a feature matrix that the different ai-models can use

5. evaluate model performance using standardized metrics such as Mean Percentage Error (MPE), Accuracy, and F1-Score, for both price change prediction (regression) and market direction classification (up/down).

6. analyze Feature Contribution via statistical tests and model weight analysis

\chapter{Literature Review}
The idea to combine market sentiment with stochastic and technical indicators is not new. Many studies have already done research on it \cite{ANBAEEFARIMANI2022108742} \cite{10380556} \cite{9175549}. The combination of sentiment and well known indicators like ballinger bands, volume, EMA (weighted moving average) are used in \cite{ANBAEEFARIMANI2022108742} to predict the price changes in Forex (Foreign exchange markets), with good performance. Similarly \cite{9175549} tried using daily aggregated sentiment and stock-data(isn´t further explained) to predict price changes of certain companies like Apple, Amazon or Microsoft. Both works have proofen a correlation between stock price and sentiment, which lead to increased model performance. Also noteable is that \cite{ANBAEEFARIMANI2022108742} stated that specifically combining both technical indicators and sentiment lead to the most performant model, hinting possible feature synergies or complimentations.

However, newspaper headlines are not the only reliable source, social media posts (e.g. twitter or social hubs) can also be utilized to create sentiment features. For example \cite{CHOI2024121589} used properties like views, likes, dislikes, and keywords to predict price momentum (up, down). Other studies like \cite{khan2022stock} even go a step further by combining both news paper headlines with social media content to create more robust accuracy.

In comparision to others this study will focus not on single companies or foreign exchange markets but broad stock indices. It is expected that indices like S\&P 500 or MSCI-Word are going to be harder to predict, because they are influenced by a broader range of factors.

\chapter{Methodology}
The fundamental data of this work will be a big corpus of news paper headlines, that combine multiple american media sources (CNBC, Reuters, The Guradians, ...). A pipeline that combines many of those datasets will be implemented. Primary goal ist to unify many different datasets, remove duplicates and create a time series with news information.
On the other hand using investpy or other public APIs (Alpha Vantage) stock and volume data will be collected for the target index. The frequency will be adjusted for the granuality of the sentiment data (for example an intra day sentiment score is only possible if enough data can be collected).

\section{First prototype}
In the course of a minimal experiment, ARIMA-Models produced hints that sentiment has a statistical value for predicitng prices. For this purpose, an autoregressive (AR) model with a moving average (MA) component was used. ARIMA models are primary used in statistic and economic szenarios. It is important to note that, in this case the previous proposed matrix was not used. Instead only price and sentiment of prior N days where utilized for predicting future values.

A ARIMA(p,d,q) model has 3 different parameter \cite{WikipediaARIMA}:
\begin{itemize}
    \item p => N past datapoints (t-1, t-2, ... t-n)
    \item d => degree of differencing, makes changes stationary (e.g., from price movement to price change)
    \item q => N past regression errors
\end{itemize}
